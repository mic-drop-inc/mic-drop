# Impromptu Speaking — Judge Prep Guide

> **High School & Middle School** | 7 minutes total (includes prep time) | No notes during speech | 3 prompts, choose 1

## Event Overview

Impromptu is the ultimate test of a student's ability to think on their feet. Students draw **3 prompts**, choose **1**, and have a total of **7 minutes** — which they split between **preparation** and **delivery** however they choose. Most competitive students take 1–2 minutes of prep and speak for 5–6 minutes.

**Types of prompts include:**
- Famous quotes (historical or modern)
- Lyrics from songs
- Proverbs or aphorisms
- Historical figures or events
- Abstract concepts or single words
- Fictional characters
- Political speeches or soundbites

**Speech Structure:**
1. **Introduction**: Creative hook → State the prompt → Central theme/thesis
2. **Body**: 2–3 main areas (stories, examples, historical references, current events)
3. **Conclusion**: Ties body back to the prompt; memorable clincher

**Key skills this event tests:**
- Dynamic verbal delivery
- Quick critical thinking
- Creative interpretation of prompts
- Broad general knowledge
- Logical organization under pressure

## Judging Criteria

### 1. Prompt Relevance & Interpretation
**What to evaluate:** Did the student actually connect their speech to the prompt they chose? Is the interpretation creative and defensible, or a stretch?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Clear, defensible connection between prompt and speech theme; creative but logical interpretation; callback to prompt throughout |
| **Good** | Connection is made but may feel thin in places; prompt mentioned in intro and conclusion |
| **Needs Work** | Prompt seems abandoned after the first sentence; speech feels generic; interpretation is a stretch or non-existent |

---

### 2. Organization & Structure
**What to evaluate:** Is the speech clearly organized despite the limited prep? Can you identify the introduction, distinct body points, and a conclusion?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Clear intro/preview, distinct body points with natural transitions, deliberate conclusion; structure feels intentional, not accidental |
| **Good** | Structure mostly present; some transitions are abrupt; conclusion may feel rushed |
| **Needs Work** | Hard to identify structure; body feels like a stream of consciousness; no real introduction or conclusion |

---

### 3. Development & Analysis
**What to evaluate:** Does the student go beyond surface-level observations? Do they develop ideas with examples, anecdotes, or evidence? Do they reveal insight about the prompt?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Ideas are developed with specific examples, stories, or references; genuine insight or original angle on the prompt; goes beyond the obvious |
| **Good** | Ideas are supported with examples; mostly developed; some points could go deeper |
| **Needs Work** | Ideas stated but not developed; relies on vague generalities; no specific examples or evidence; says "this is important" without explaining why |

---

### 4. Creativity & Originality
**What to evaluate:** Does the student find an interesting, unexpected angle on the prompt? Impromptu rewards surprising, creative interpretations more than obvious ones.

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Surprising and compelling interpretation; audience thinks "I wouldn't have seen it that way"; fresh perspective |
| **Good** | Solid but somewhat predictable take; creative elements present |
| **Needs Work** | Completely predictable; if you heard the prompt, you knew exactly what they'd say; no creative risk taken |

---

### 5. Vocal & Physical Delivery
**What to evaluate:** For a limited-prep event, delivery should still be engaging. Voice, eye contact, and physical presence matter.

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Confident and conversational; eye contact throughout; vocal variety and natural pace; physical presence enhances the speech |
| **Good** | Generally clear and audible; some filler words; decent eye contact |
| **Needs Work** | Monotone; excessive filler words; looking away; rigid physical presence; sounds like they're searching for their next thought constantly |

---

### 6. Overall Effect
**What to evaluate:** Did the speech leave you with something — a new perspective, a laugh, a takeaway? Was it engaging and memorable?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Memorable; you walked away thinking about the prompt differently; engaging from start to finish |
| **Good** | Solid and engaging; some memorable moments |
| **Needs Work** | Forgettable; hard to stay engaged; no clear takeaway |

---

## Things to Watch For as a Judge

- **Time management:** The 7-minute total includes prep. Watch for students who took too much prep time and then had to rush the speech, or vice versa. Note if the speech is very short (under 4–5 minutes of actual speaking).
- **No notes during speech:** Students must deliver without notes after prep. If a student uses notes or reads from their prep paper during the speech, that's a violation — note it.
- **Prompt connection:** The most common weakness is abandoning the prompt. Look for whether the connection is maintained throughout, not just referenced at the start.
- **Generic speeches:** Some students have pre-written Impromptu "templates" that they adapt regardless of the prompt. If the speech feels suspiciously polished and generic, consider whether genuine engagement with the prompt is happening.
- **Filler words:** Common in this event given the limited prep — be charitable but still note if excessive.
- **Prompt selection:** Students choose from 3 prompts. They may choose based on their prep file, which is fine and strategic.

## Sample Judge Comments

### Prompt Relevance & Interpretation

**Strong:**
- surprising, non-obvious interpretation
- fresh angle on the prompt
- connection maintained throughout

**Average:**
- prompt connected in intro only
- body feels generic, prompt-neutral
- weave prompt language throughout

**Needs Work:**
- prompt abandoned after opening
- speech could be about anything
- sustain interpretation across body

---

### Organization & Structure

**Strong:**
- clean intro with preview
- distinct body points, smooth transitions
- conclusion circles back to opening

**Average:**
- main points identifiable
- invest in clearer transitions
- add signposts between points

**Needs Work:**
- structure hard to follow
- points blur into each other
- practice an intro/body/conclusion template

---

### Development & Analysis

**Strong:**
- specific story, not just mention
- explains why it connects
- rare depth and specificity

**Average:**
- points solid but thin
- stated idea then moved on
- add a specific example each point

**Needs Work:**
- stayed at surface level
- "important" without showing why
- back each point with evidence

---

### Creativity & Originality

**Strong:**
- genuinely creative angle
- went somewhere unexpected
- creative risk made it memorable

**Average:**
- solid but appropriate take
- seek the less-obvious path
- aim for unexpected interpretation

**Needs Work:**
- followed the predictable path
- no creative risk taken
- prep for least-obvious connection

---

### Vocal & Physical Delivery

**Strong:**
- confident and conversational
- consistent eye contact
- pace lets ideas land

**Average:**
- solid delivery overall
- filler words at transitions
- make physical presence intentional

**Needs Work:**
- many filler words, sounds uncertain
- looking away breaks connection
- rehearse transitions in prep

---

### Overall Effect

**Strong:**
- impressive across the board
- left me with new perspective
- creative, structured, confident

**Average:**
- solid with real strengths
- tighten prompt connection
- develop each point more specifically

**Needs Work:**
- real courage speaking on the spot
- use prep time to set structure
- commit to specific examples

---

## Quick Reference Checklist

- [ ] Prompt clearly connected to speech (intro AND body AND conclusion)
- [ ] No notes used during speech (prep paper stays at seat)
- [ ] Clear introduction with hook and thesis
- [ ] 2–3 distinct body points with transitions
- [ ] Each point developed with specific examples/stories/evidence
- [ ] Conclusion ties back to prompt with clincher
- [ ] Within 7-minute total (including prep time)
- [ ] Vocal delivery: audible, varied, minimal filler
- [ ] Eye contact maintained throughout
- [ ] Creative interpretation of prompt (not just the obvious angle)
