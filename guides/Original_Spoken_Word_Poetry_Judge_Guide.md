# Original Spoken Word Poetry — Judge Prep Guide

> **Supplemental Event** | Max 5 minutes + 30-second grace | Memorized, no book or script | No props or costumes

## Event Overview

In Original Spoken Word Poetry (OSWP), the student **writes and performs their own original poem**. Unlike interp Poetry — which performs *published* work from a binder — OSWP is the performer's own words, delivered **from memory** with no book or script. The poem is created to be performed *aloud*: it lives in rhythm, sound, imagery, and the performer's authentic voice. At its core, the event is about heart — the genuine emotions, experiences, and opinions of the performer. Topics often address what directly affects the poet: identity, family, social justice, race, gender, community, mental health, and more.

This is the key distinction for judges: **you are rewarding both the writing and the performance.** A strong piece reflects the performer's unique lived experience and outlook, then delivers it with the dynamic, spoken-word craft the form is built on (think rap, hip hop, storytelling, rhyme, repetition, and improvisation).

**Structure:**
- **Introduction** (optional): Permitted but not required. May frame the topic or set context. Many poets open directly with the poem.
- **Gateway line:** The line — often near the start — that tells the audience what the poem is *about* and hooks them. The rest of the poem reinforces and expands from it.
- **Body / build:** The poem develops its message through vivid imagery, a recurring **motif**, and sound, building toward a climax.
- **Power line / closing:** One or more impactful lines that stick with the audience. The final line should land the message and leave the audience thinking past the end of the performance.

**Key rules to know:**
- The poem must be **original** — written by the performer.
- Maximum time limit is **5 minutes with a 30-second grace period**.
- Delivery must be **memorized**; **no book or script may be used**.
- **No more than 150 words** of the poem may be direct quotation from any other speech or writing, and such quotations **must be identified in a printed copy** of the poem.
- An introduction is **permitted but not required**.
- At supplemental events, performances are evaluated on **performance, vocalization, and originality**.

## Judging Criteria

### 1. Originality & Authorial Voice
**What to evaluate:** Is this clearly the performer's own writing and lived experience? Does the piece feel authentic and personal rather than borrowed, generic, or imitative? (Remember: no more than 150 quoted words, and those must be flagged in a printed copy.)

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Unmistakably personal; rooted in the poet's own experience and outlook; a voice you couldn't mistake for anyone else's |
| **Good** | Original and sincere, but voice occasionally drifts toward generic or familiar phrasing |
| **Needs Work** | Feels borrowed, clichéd, or impersonal; leans on others' ideas; little sense of who the poet is |

---

### 2. Message & Theme
**What to evaluate:** Is there a clear, meaningful message? Does the gateway line orient the audience early, and does the poem stay cohesive and purposeful around its theme?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Clear theme established early; every section reinforces it; the audience sees the big picture and feels the point |
| **Good** | Message comes through, but focus wanders in places or the gateway is slow to arrive |
| **Needs Work** | Theme is muddled or absent; poem feels like disconnected fragments; unclear what it's about or why it matters |

---

### 3. Literary & Poetic Craft
**What to evaluate:** Imagery, metaphor, wordplay, and sound devices (rhyme, repetition, alliteration, assonance, onomatopoeia). Are sensory details vivid? Does wordplay add depth without muddling the message?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Vivid, sensory imagery; fresh metaphor; purposeful wordplay and sound devices that deepen meaning |
| **Good** | Some strong images and devices, but uneven — moments of cliché, or wordplay that occasionally clouds the message |
| **Needs Work** | Flat or literal language; little imagery; devices absent, or so dense the meaning is lost |

---

### 4. Structure & Build
**What to evaluate:** Does the poem have an arc? Does it build toward a climax through motif and recurring lines? Does humor or tonal variation create dynamic levels?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Clear build to a climax; motif woven throughout; tonal levels (including well-placed humor) keep it dynamic; strong final line |
| **Good** | Discernible arc, but the build is gentle or the ending under-delivers; motif used but inconsistently |
| **Needs Work** | Flat or aimless; no sense of building; repetitive without purpose; ending fizzles |

---

### 5. Vocal Delivery & Cadence
**What to evaluate:** Pacing, tone, rhythm/flow, projection, and enunciation. Does the voice match the emotion? Are power lines given room to land? Is every carefully chosen word heard?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Rhythmic flow that serves the piece; deliberate shifts in pace and volume; strong projection from the diaphragm; crisp enunciation; power lines land |
| **Good** | Generally clear and well-paced; some variation, but occasional rushing, dropped words, or monotone stretches |
| **Needs Work** | Monotone, mumbled, or so fast words are lost; flat cadence; projection or enunciation undercuts the message |

---

### 6. Physical Delivery & Presence
**What to evaluate:** Eye contact, facial expression, gestures, posture. Does the body reinforce the words? Is eye contact meaningful (held through a thought) rather than scanning?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Meaningful, sustained eye contact; expressive face and eyes; bold, natural gestures that match the tone of voice |
| **Good** | Solid presence and eye contact; gestures present but sometimes rigid, repetitive, or mismatched to the words |
| **Needs Work** | Minimal eye contact or constant scanning; flat expression; gestures distracting, absent, or disconnected from meaning |

---

### 7. Overall Effect / Impact
**What to evaluate:** Did the piece elicit critical thought, reflection, or emotion? Did the writing and the performance combine to move you?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Memorable and resonant; writing and delivery work as one; the message stays with you past the final line |
| **Good** | Communicates and connects; some genuine emotion; impact uneven across the piece |
| **Needs Work** | Falls flat or forgettable; unclear takeaway; writing and delivery don't yet reinforce each other |

---

## Things to Watch For as a Judge

- **Is it original and the performer's own?** This is the heart of the event. If a piece feels lifted or impersonal, note it. Reward authentic, lived voice. (OSWP is original and self-written — unlike interp Poetry, which performs published work from a binder.)
- **Quotation rule:** No more than 150 words may be direct quotation from another work, and those must be identified in a printed copy. You will rarely police this directly, but be aware of it.
- **Writing *and* performance:** Evaluate both. A beautifully written poem delivered flatly, or a dazzling delivery of thin writing, are each incomplete. The best pieces marry the two.
- **Memorization:** Delivery must be memorized — no book or script. Minor stumbles are common and don't need comment unless they significantly disrupt the piece. (Note: improvising or "freestyling" to keep the flow is a legitimate spoken-word skill, not a memorization failure.)
- **Time violations:** Over 5 minutes plus the 30-second grace is a rules issue; note it on the ballot.
- **Don't judge the opinion, judge the craft.** Topics are personal and often raw. Evaluate how effectively the message is written and delivered — not whether you agree.
- **Wordplay overload:** Lots of clever wordplay isn't automatically better. If the meaning gets buried, that's a flaw worth naming.

## Sample Judge Comments

### Originality & Authorial Voice

**Strong:**
- unmistakably your own voice
- rooted in lived experience
- authenticity makes it land

**Average:**
- sincerity comes through
- some generic phrasing
- push toward specific lived details

**Needs Work:**
- feels borrowed, not personal
- ideas heard, not lived
- write from your own experience

---

### Message & Theme

**Strong:**
- gateway line oriented me immediately
- every section reinforced the theme
- clear what it's about and why

**Average:**
- message there but slow to surface
- focus wandered in the middle
- establish theme earlier

**Needs Work:**
- hard to identify the theme
- reads as disconnected moments
- find your gateway line

---

### Literary & Poetic Craft

**Strong:**
- vivid, sensory imagery
- fresh metaphors
- wordplay deepens meaning

**Average:**
- some striking images
- literal lines dilute it
- wordplay briefly clouds message

**Needs Work:**
- language stayed mostly literal
- tells rather than shows
- lean into sensory detail

---

### Structure & Build

**Strong:**
- motif recurred and gained weight
- tonal shifts kept it dynamic
- final line pulled it together

**Average:**
- clear arc but gentle build
- ending under-delivered
- find a closing power line

**Needs Work:**
- felt level throughout
- no sense of building
- thread a motif, shape a rising arc

---

### Vocal Delivery & Cadence

**Strong:**
- cadence served the poem
- deliberate pace and volume shifts
- power lines landed, every word heard

**Average:**
- clear and mostly well-paced
- key lines rushed, words swallowed
- slow down on the moments that matter

**Needs Work:**
- one pace and pitch throughout
- flattened the emotion
- vary pace and volume to match feeling

---

### Physical Delivery & Presence

**Strong:**
- eye contact held a full thought
- emotion felt direct and personal
- bold, natural gestures matched tone

**Average:**
- solid presence
- eye contact scans rather than settles
- let gestures arise from the words

**Needs Work:**
- looking past the audience
- expression stayed fixed
- use eyes and face to tell the story

---

### Overall Effect

**Strong:**
- memorable and resonant
- writing and performance reinforced each other
- message stayed past the final line

**Average:**
- solid, sincere, connected
- impact uneven across the piece
- bring strongest commitment to every line

**Needs Work:**
- foundation is here
- doesn't make us feel it yet
- find the emotional core, let it drive every line

---

## Quick Reference Checklist

- [ ] Poem is original — the performer's own writing
- [ ] Memorized; no book or script used
- [ ] Within 5 minutes (+ 30-second grace)
- [ ] Clear gateway line and meaningful theme
- [ ] Vivid imagery, metaphor, and purposeful wordplay
- [ ] Sound devices (rhythm, repetition, rhyme) serve the message
- [ ] Builds toward a climax; strong final / power line
- [ ] Vocal variety, projection, and clean enunciation
- [ ] Meaningful eye contact and expressive physical presence
- [ ] Authentic personal voice (writing *and* performance reward)
