# Declamation — Judge Prep Guide

> **Middle School Event** | Max 10 minutes | No props or costumes

## Event Overview

In Declamation, students perform an excerpt from a **previously delivered public speech** by a real speaker. The student is NOT impersonating the original speaker — they are re-delivering the message through their own voice and interpretation. The speech should feel authentic, not like an impression.

**Structure:**
- **Teaser** (30–45 sec): A captivating opening moment from the piece, before the intro
- **Introduction** (20–30 sec): Names the speech, occasion, original speaker, and why it matters
- **Main Body** (7–8 min): The performance of the cutting
- **Conclusion** (30–45 sec): Wraps up the message, returns to teaser

**Key rules to know:**
- Original speech must have been delivered publicly (convention, commencement, TED Talk, etc.)
- No mimicking the original speaker's voice, mannerisms, or ethnicity-specific delivery
- No props or costumes
- Time limit: 10 minutes (timekeeper typically signals at 9:30 and 10:00)

## Judging Criteria

### 1. Selection & Cutting Quality
**What to evaluate:** Did the student choose a speech with a clear, meaningful message? Is the cutting well-structured with a beginning, middle, and end? Does it work as a standalone piece?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Timely, meaningful speech; cutting flows naturally; message is clear and complete; appropriate for the student's age |
| **Good** | Solid selection; minor gaps in cutting structure; message mostly comes through |
| **Needs Work** | Obscure or inappropriately complex speech; cutting feels incomplete or choppy; message is muddled |

---

### 2. Introduction
**What to evaluate:** Does the intro name the original speech, speaker, and occasion? Does it contextualize the piece and draw the audience in?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | All required info included naturally; compelling hook; audience understands stakes before body begins |
| **Good** | All info present but intro feels formulaic or rushed; minimal engagement |
| **Needs Work** | Missing key info (title, speaker, occasion); intro disconnected from the body |

---

### 3. Vocal Delivery
**What to evaluate:** Rate, pitch, volume, articulation, and use of pause. Does their voice serve the message?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Dynamic range; meaningful pauses; words land with clarity and intention; pacing matches emotional tone |
| **Good** | Mostly clear; some variation; occasional rushing or monotone stretches |
| **Needs Work** | Monotone, mumbled, too fast/slow; hard to follow; voice doesn't convey emotion |

---

### 4. Physical Delivery
**What to evaluate:** Eye contact, gestures, posture, facial expression, movement. Does their body reinforce the message?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Natural, purposeful movement; strong eye contact; gestures match words; facial expressions alive |
| **Good** | Decent posture and eye contact; some gestures but occasional rigidity or nervous habits |
| **Needs Work** | Reading posture, minimal eye contact, distracting habits (swaying, fidgeting, locked knees) |

---

### 5. Connection to Content / Authenticity
**What to evaluate:** Does the student believe the words they're saying? Is it a re-delivery (their own voice) or an imitation?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Student owns the message; delivery feels personal and genuine; emotional commitment throughout |
| **Good** | Mostly believable; occasional moments that feel rehearsed or hollow |
| **Needs Work** | Feels like recitation; student is mimicking original speaker; no personal investment visible |

---

### 6. Overall Effect / Impact
**What to evaluate:** Did the performance move you? Did you understand and feel the original message?

| Level | Indicators |
|-------|-----------|
| **Excellent** | Memorable; emotionally resonant; left a clear impression; message stayed with you |
| **Good** | Solid performance; message communicated; some emotional connection |
| **Needs Work** | Performance felt flat or forgettable; unclear takeaway |

---

## Things to Watch For as a Judge

- **Is the student mimicking the original speaker?** If yes, that's a flaw — note it. Declamation is re-delivery, not impersonation.
- **Does the intro include all required elements?** (title, original speaker, occasion/context)
- **Time violations:** Over 10 minutes is a rules violation; note it on the ballot.
- **Memorization lapses:** Minor stumbles are common and don't require comment unless they significantly disrupt the piece.
- **Age-appropriate content:** Since this is a middle school event, flag anything inappropriately mature.
- **Teaser structure:** Is there a clear teaser before the intro, or do they go straight into the body?

## Sample Judge Comments

### Selection & Cutting

**Strong:**
- excellent, timely selection
- complete narrative arc
- natural beginning and satisfying close

**Average:**
- powerful message, choppy cutting
- add transitions to bridge gaps

**Needs Work:**
- overly familiar, often-performed piece
- cutting adds nothing new
- find a fresher selection

---

### Introduction

**Strong:**
- smooth, informative intro
- speaker, occasion, and why clear
- teaser pulled me in

**Average:**
- covers basics but rushed
- let the context land

**Needs Work:**
- missing occasion or speaker
- include who, when, and why
- give needed context first

---

### Vocal Delivery

**Strong:**
- outstanding vocal range
- pace and volume create tension
- well-timed pause before final line

**Average:**
- clear articulation
- pace and volume too consistent
- vary loud and quiet moments

**Needs Work:**
- rushed key moments
- hard to follow at times
- write in intentional pauses

---

### Physical Delivery

**Strong:**
- strong eye contact across room
- felt like direct conversation
- natural, non-distracting gestures

**Average:**
- mostly good eye contact
- gaze drops on transitions
- make gestures more intentional

**Needs Work:**
- focused on one part of room
- distribute eye contact broadly
- match gestures to meaning

---

### Connection / Authenticity

**Strong:**
- truly owned the speech
- never felt like reciting
- rare, elevating authenticity

**Average:**
- genuine connection at climax
- opening and closing need more investment

**Needs Work:**
- felt like recitation
- find your own reason for every word
- make this speech your own

---

### Overall Effect

**Strong:**
- memorable performance
- powerful piece made your own
- engaging vocal and physical control

**Average:**
- solid with clear strengths
- land the key moments
- more commitment to go from good to great

**Needs Work:**
- foundation and structure in place
- make the audience feel something
- find the emotional core

---

## Quick Reference Checklist

- [ ] Teaser present before introduction
- [ ] Introduction names speech, speaker, and occasion
- [ ] No mimicry of original speaker
- [ ] Clear message / argument throughout
- [ ] Vocal variety (rate, pitch, volume, pause)
- [ ] Strong eye contact and physical presence
- [ ] Authentic re-delivery (not recitation)
- [ ] Within 10-minute time limit
