## Critical Rules at a Glance

### Universal Rules (All Events)
- Judges **rank** competitors (1 = best) and write comments on ballots
- Note time violations on the ballot — tab handles consequences
- Do not coach or signal students during the round

### Common Violations to Flag on Ballot
- **Props or costumes** in events where prohibited (DI, HI, DUO)
- **Direct eye contact between partners** in DUO (off-stage focus required)
- **Notes during speech** in IMP (no notes after prep) or OSWP (must be memorized)
- **Manuscript in events requiring memorization** (IMP, OSWP, STO)
- **No manuscript in events requiring one** (POE, PRO, POI)
- **Visual aids in Expository** (not permitted at Nationals)
- **Performers touching** in DUO (no physical contact)

### NSDA Extemp — Key Distinction
- **USX (US Extemporaneous):** US domestic policy, politics, economy, social issues
- **IX (International Extemporaneous):** International affairs, foreign policy, global events
- Both: 30 min prep, 7 min speech, internet allowed at Nationals

## Judging Criteria Summary by Event Type

### Interpretation Events (DI, HI, DUO, POI, POE, PRO, STO)
1. **Literature / Cutting Quality** — Is the material appropriate and well-structured?
2. **Introduction** — Names work/author; sets up stakes
3. **Characterization** — Distinct, consistent, motivated characters
4. **Blocking / Physical Performance** — Off-stage focus, space use, bookwork (if applicable)
5. **Vocal Performance** — Range, expressiveness, timing
6. **Overall Effect** — Did it land emotionally?

*DUO adds:* Relationship/Partnership, Balance between performers
*POI adds:* Thematic Argument, Threading/Transitions, Genre Balance
*HI adds:* Comedic Timing (critical)

### Public Speaking Events (OO, INFO, EXP, DEC)
1. **Topic / Content** — Fresh, appropriate, in-scope
2. **Research / Evidence** — Specific, cited, integrated
3. **Organization / Structure** — Clear thesis, transitions, conclusion
4. **Language / Writing** — Voice, precision, clarity
5. **Vocal Delivery** — Dynamic, engaging
6. **Physical Delivery** — Eye contact, gesture, presence
7. **Overall Effect** — Educational/persuasive impact

*INFO adds:* Must be informative NOT persuasive (no advocacy)
*OO adds:* Argumentative logic and rhetorical power

### Limited Prep Events (USX, IX, IMP)
1. **Answering the Prompt** — Directly and fully
2. **Organization** — Clear structure despite limited prep
3. **Evidence / Development** — Specific examples, citations (Extemp: sources required)
4. **Analysis** — Goes beyond facts to meaning
5. **Delivery** — Confident under pressure
6. **Overall Effect** — Intellectually/rhetorically compelling

### Spoken Word Poetry (OSWP)
Evaluated explicitly on three criteria per NSDA rules:
1. **Performance** — Physical embodiment of the poem
2. **Vocalization** — Rhythmic, dynamic vocal delivery
3. **Originality** — Quality and freshness of the student-written poetry

## Judging Mindset

**For interpretation events:** Ask yourself — did I believe the characters? Did the story affect me?

**For public speaking events:** Ask yourself — did I learn something / change my mind? Was the argument earned?

**For limited prep events:** Ask yourself — did they actually answer the question? Was the analysis genuine?

**When ranking is hard:** Look for the performance that took the most creative risk and executed it best. Safe performances at Nationals rarely win.

**On filler words and small stumbles:** In Impromptu and Extemp, be charitable — this is limited prep. In prepared events, note if excessive.

**Write comments for every criterion.** The ballot is feedback students use to grow. A ranking without comments is a missed opportunity.

---

## Sample Ballot Comment Format

> **[Criterion]:** [Specific observation] — [why it matters or what to work on].

Example:
> **Characterization:** Your distinction between the mother and daughter was crystalline — I knew exactly who was speaking from the first word. The grandmother character could use more physical specificity to match the strong vocal choice. In competition, that third layer of differentiation is often the deciding factor.

Keep comments:
- **Specific** (name the moment, name the choice)
- **Honest** (don't inflate weak performances)
- **Constructive** (tell them what to work on, not just what's wrong)
- **Encouraging** (especially for MS events — these are young students)
